Parabola and Sine Path Construction

\draw (0,0) rectangle (1,1) (0,0) parabola (1,1);

\draw[x=1pt,y=1pt] (0,0) parabola bend (4,16) (6,12);

\draw[x=1.57ex,y=1ex] (0,0) sin (1,1) cos (2,0) sin (3,-1) cos (4,0)

Cut piece of the graph

\clip (-0.1,-0.2) rectangle (1.1,0.75);

Filling and Drawing

\fill[green!20!white] (0,0) -- (3mm,0mm) arc (0:30:3mm) -- (0,0);

\fill[green!20!white] (0,0) -- (3mm,0mm) arc (0:30:3mm) -- cycle;

Here green!20!white means 20% green mixed with 80%white.

Shading

\shade[left color=yellow, right color=black] (3,0) rectangle +(2,1);

\shadedraw[inner color=yellow, outer color=black,draw=yellow] (6,0) rectangle +(2,1);

\shade[ball color=green] (9,.5) circle (.5cm);

Specifyint Coordinates

(10pt, 2cm)

(1,2) means "one times the current x-vector plub twice the current y-vector". There vectors default to 1cm respectively.

(30:1cm) means 1cm in direction 30 degree.

+(1cm,0cm) means 1cm upwards from the previous specified position

++(0cm,2cm) means 2cm to the right of the previous specified position, making this new specified position.

(intersection of 1,0 -- 1,1 and 0,0 -- 30:1cm)

(30:1cm |- 0,0) means the intersection of a vertical line through p and a horizontal line through q.

Adding Arrow Tips

\draw [->] (-1.5,0) -- (1.5,0);

\draw [<->] (0,0) arc (180:30:10pt);

\begin{tikzpicture} [>=steelth] %steelth is a kind of arrow
\draw [<->] (1,0) -- (1.5cm,10pt) -- (2cm,0pt) -- (2.5cm,10pt);
\draw [<<-,very thick] (1,0) -- (1.5cm,10pt) -- (2cm,0pt) -- (2.5cm,10pt);
\end{tikzpicture}
